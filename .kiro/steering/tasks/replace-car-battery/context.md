## Overview

This steering file defines the context for replacing the car battery, including objectives, success criteria, vehicle-specific details, and constraints. This task demonstrates the complete reference chain from task to project to household to framework.

## Task Objectives

### Primary Objective

Replace the aging car battery before winter weather arrives to ensure reliable vehicle starting and prevent being stranded.

### Secondary Objectives

- **Learn DIY Battery Replacement**: Build confidence and knowledge for future battery replacements
- **Cost Savings**: Save $50-100 by performing replacement myself vs. paying shop labor
- **Proper Disposal**: Ensure old battery is recycled properly and core charge is recovered

## Success Criteria

- **Battery Installed Correctly**: New battery installed with proper polarity, tight connections, and secure hold-down
  - Measurable: Vehicle starts normally, no warning lights, all accessories function
  
- **Safety Maintained**: All safety procedures followed, no injuries or damage
  - Measurable: No acid spills, no electrical shorts, no personal injury

- **Old Battery Recycled**: Old battery returned to auto parts store for recycling and core charge refund
  - Measurable: Core charge refund received, recycling receipt obtained

- **Documentation Complete**: Battery replacement documented in maintenance log with date, mileage, and part numbers
  - Measurable: Maintenance log entry created with all required information

- **Cost Within Budget**: Total cost (battery + supplies) stays within $150 budget
  - Measurable: Receipt total ≤ $150

## Vehicle-Specific Details

### Vehicle Information

- **Make/Model/Year**: Honda Civic 2015
- **Current Mileage**: 85,000 miles
- **Battery Location**: Engine bay, driver's side
- **Current Battery Age**: 6 years (original battery)

### Battery Specifications

- **Group Size**: 51R (Honda/Acura standard)
- **CCA Required**: 410 CCA minimum (per owner's manual)
- **CCA Target**: 500+ CCA (20% above minimum for cold weather reliability)
- **Terminal Type**: Top post
- **Dimensions**: 9.4" L x 5.1" W x 8.9" H

### Current Symptoms

- **Slow Cranking**: Engine cranks slowly on cold mornings (below 40°F)
- **Voltage Drop**: Battery voltage drops to 11.8V overnight (should be 12.4V+)
- **Age**: 6 years old, approaching end of typical lifespan (4-6 years)
- **No Warning Light**: Battery light not illuminated (alternator charging properly)

## Prerequisites

### Required Before Starting

- **Battery Purchased**: New battery acquired with correct specifications

  - CCA: 500+

- **Weather Suitable**: Dry weather, temperature above 40°F
  - Check forecast: No rain expected during work window

- **Time Available**: 1-2 hours available without interruption
  - No household commitments during work window
  - Vehicle not needed for transportation during work

- **Memory Saver Ready**: 9V battery memory saver device or radio code available
  - Prevents loss of radio presets and clock settings
  - Radio security code: [Record code from owner's manual]

## Risk Mitigation

### Identified Risks

- **Electrical Short**: Risk of creating spark or short circuit
  - Mitigation: Follow disconnect/connect sequence exactly (negative first off, positive first on)
  - Mitigation: Keep metal tools away from both terminals simultaneously

- **Acid Exposure**: Risk of battery acid contact with skin or eyes
  - Mitigation: Wear safety glasses and gloves at all times
  - Mitigation: Have water available for rinsing if contact occurs

- **Memory Loss**: Risk of losing vehicle settings and radio code
  - Mitigation: Use memory saver device if available
  - Mitigation: Have radio security code written down before starting

- **Wrong Battery**: Risk of purchasing incorrect battery size or specifications
  - Mitigation: Verify Group 51R and 500+ CCA before purchase
  - Mitigation: Bring old battery to store for comparison

- **Injury from Lifting**: Risk of back injury from improper lifting
  - Mitigation: Use proper lifting technique (bend knees, not back)
  - Mitigation: Take time, don't rush the lift
