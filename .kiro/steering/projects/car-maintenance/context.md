## Project Goals

### Primary Objectives

- **Maintain Vehicle Reliability**: Keep vehicle in good working condition to prevent breakdowns
  - Success Criteria: No unexpected breakdowns or failures
  - Success Criteria: All scheduled maintenance completed on time

- **Extend Vehicle Lifespan**: Perform preventive maintenance to maximize vehicle longevity
  - Success Criteria: Vehicle operates reliably for 200,000+ miles
  - Success Criteria: Major components (engine, transmission) last full expected lifespan

- **Ensure Safety**: Maintain all safety-critical systems in proper working order
  - Success Criteria: All safety systems (brakes, lights, tires) pass inspection
  - Success Criteria: No safety-related warning lights on dashboard

### Secondary Objectives

- **Cost Management**: Minimize long-term maintenance costs through preventive care
  - Success Criteria: Avoid major repairs through regular maintenance
  - Success Criteria: Track maintenance costs and stay within budget

- **DIY Capability**: Perform routine maintenance tasks independently when possible
  - Success Criteria: Successfully complete basic maintenance (oil changes, battery replacement, air filters)
  - Success Criteria: Build knowledge and confidence for future maintenance

## Stakeholders

### Primary Stakeholders

- **Vehicle Owner**: Primary user, responsible for maintenance decisions and costs
  - Interest: Reliable transportation, cost-effective maintenance
  - Involvement: Approves all maintenance work and expenses

- **Household Members**: Secondary users who depend on vehicle reliability
  - Interest: Safe and reliable transportation
  - Involvement: Provide input on vehicle issues and needs

### Secondary Stakeholders

- **Mechanic/Service Provider**: Performs maintenance work beyond DIY capability
  - Interest: Quality work, customer satisfaction
  - Involvement: Consulted for complex repairs and diagnostics

- **Auto Parts Supplier**: Provides parts and materials for maintenance
  - Interest: Parts sales, customer loyalty
  - Involvement: Source for parts, advice on specifications

## Constraints

### Time Constraints

- **Weekend Work**: Most maintenance must be performed on weekends
- **Weather Dependent**: Outdoor work limited by weather conditions
- **Availability**: Limited time for complex repairs (2-4 hours maximum per session)

### Budget Constraints

- **Monthly Budget**: $100-150 for routine maintenance
- **Emergency Fund**: $500 available for unexpected repairs
- **Annual Budget**: $1,500-2,000 for all maintenance and repairs
- **Cost Approval**: Expenses over $200 require household budget review

### Resource Constraints

- **Tools**: Basic tool set available (wrenches, sockets, screwdrivers)
- **Workspace**: Driveway available, no garage or lift
- **Knowledge**: Moderate DIY skill level, learning as needed
- **Assistance**: Limited - mostly solo work

### Technical Constraints

- **DIY Limitations**: Complex diagnostics or specialized tools require professional service
- **Warranty Considerations**: Some work must be performed by certified mechanic to maintain warranty
- **Safety Requirements**: Safety-critical systems (brakes, steering) require professional verification

### Other Constraints

- **Parts Availability**: Some parts may require ordering (1-3 day lead time)
- **Disposal**: Used fluids and parts must be disposed of properly (recycling center trips)
- **Documentation**: Maintenance records must be kept for resale value

## Dependencies

- **Weather**: Outdoor maintenance dependent on dry, moderate temperature conditions
- **Parts Availability**: Work cannot proceed without correct parts in hand
- **Tool Access**: Some tasks require borrowing or renting specialized tools
- **Knowledge**: May need to research procedures or watch tutorials before starting
- **Household Schedule**: Work must fit around household activities and vehicle usage needs

## Maintenance Schedule

### Routine Maintenance

- **Oil Change**: Every 5,000 miles or 6 months
- **Tire Rotation**: Every 7,500 miles or 6 months
- **Air Filter**: Every 15,000 miles or 12 months
- **Cabin Air Filter**: Every 15,000 miles or 12 months
- **Battery Check**: Every 6 months (voltage and terminal condition)

### Periodic Maintenance

- **Brake Inspection**: Every 12 months or 15,000 miles
- **Coolant Flush**: Every 30,000 miles or 2 years
- **Transmission Fluid**: Every 60,000 miles or 3 years
- **Spark Plugs**: Every 100,000 miles

### Seasonal Maintenance

- **Winter Prep**: Battery check, antifreeze level, tire pressure (October)
- **Summer Prep**: Cooling system check, A/C performance, tire condition (April)

## Notes

This project is ongoing and will continue as long as the vehicle is in service. Regular maintenance is prioritized over cosmetic repairs. All maintenance is documented in a maintenance log for tracking and resale value.

Vehicle-specific information should be updated as the vehicle ages or conditions change. Budget constraints may be adjusted based on household financial situation.
